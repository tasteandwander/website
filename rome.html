Appetizers

Supplì al telefono

Golden-fried rice balls filled with rich ragù or vibrant tomato sauce, and a core of mozzarella that stretches with every bite.

Ingredients:

Rice, mozzarella, tomato sauce or ragù, eggs, breadcrumbs, olive oil

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias and street food vendors throughout Rome

Fiori di zucca

Gently stuffed with mozzarella (and anchovies), then lightly fried to a crisp perfection.

Ingredients:

Zucchini flowers, mozzarella, anchovies (optional), flour, sparkling water, olive oil

Where to taste:

Jewish Quarter (Ghetto), traditional Roman restaurants

Baccalà fritto alla romana

Cod wrapped in a light, golden batter and fried to crisp perfection.

Ingredients:

Salt cod (baccalà), flour, water, olive oil for frying

Where to taste:

Testaccio neighborhood, traditional friggitorie (fry shops)

First Course

Carbonara

Pasta entwined with a luscious, velvety sauce of eggs and Pecorino, studded with crispy, savory guanciale.

Ingredients:

Pasta (spaghetti, rigatoni or mezze maniche), Guanciale or pancetta, Eggs, Pecorino romano, Black pepper, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere

Amatriciana

Pasta bathed in tomato sauce slowly simmered to deepen its flavor, enriched by tender guanciale.

Ingredients:

Pasta, Tomato sauce, Guanciale, Pecorino cheese, Chili flakes

Where to taste:

Rome’s traditional trattorias and Osterie

Cacio e Pepe

Pasta wrapped in a creamy Pecorino emulsion with freshly cracked pepper.

Ingredients:

Pasta, Pecorino cheese, Black pepper, Pasta water

Where to taste:

Trattoria da Felice

Gricia

Pasta tossed with crisp guanciale and a generous snowfall of Pecorino.

Ingredients:

Tonnarelli pasta, Guanciale, Pecorino cheese, Black pepper

Where to taste:

Sellero e Ventresca, and trattorias

Gnocchi alla Romana

Soft semolina rounds baked until their edges turn golden, layered with butter and Parmigiano.

Ingredients:

Semolina, Butter, Egg yolk, Milk, Parmigiano, Nutmeg, Salt, Black pepper

Where to taste:

Classic Roman trattorias, especially in the Campo de' Fiori area

Rigatoni alla Pajata

Rigatoni cloaked in a rich, tangy tomato sauce infused with the unique Roman delicacy of pajata.

Ingredients:

Rigatoni, Tomato sauce, Veal intestines (pajata), Onion, Celery, White wine, Chili flakes

Where to taste:

Classic Roman trattorias, especially in the Campo de' Fiori area

Second Course

Coda alla Vaccinara

Slow-cooked oxtail melting into silky strands, in a tomato sauce that gently simmers it into something lush, and utterly comforting.

Ingredients:

Oxtail, Tomato sauce, Onion, Celery, Carrot, White wine, Black pepper, Salt, Olive oil

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere

Saltimbocca alla Romana

Thin veal kissed with sage and covered in prosciutto, gently sautéed until the edges turn golden and the flavors fuse into a delicate, savory whisper.

Ingredients:

Veal slices, Sage, Prosciutto crudo, White wine, Butter, Flour, Black pepper, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias

Trippa alla Romana

Tender ribbons of slow-cooked tripe simmered in a fragrant tomato embrace, brightened with mint and crowned with Pecorino.

Ingredients:

Belly, Pomodori San Marzano, Pecorino Romano, White onion, White wine, Carrot, Celery, Mint, Pepper, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere

Fagioli con le Cotiche

Tender beans and succulent strips of pork rind simmered together in a rich, savory sauce that clings to every bite.

Ingredients:

Borlotti beans, Pork rind, Carrots, White onions, Tomato sauce, Celery, Chili, White wine, Garlic, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere

Carciofi alla romana

Artichokes gently opened like quiet spring blossoms, filled with herbs and garlic, then braised until yielding and aromatic.

Ingredients:

Artichoke, Mint, Parsley, White wine, Lemon, Garlic, Olive oil, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere

Carciofi alla Giudia

Artichokes, tender on the inside and crisp on the outside, fried with just a touch of salt— from the Roman-Jewish culinary tradition.

Ingredients:

Artichoke, Oil, Salt

Where to taste:

Traditional Roman trattorias, especially in Trastevere
